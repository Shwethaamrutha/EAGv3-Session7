**Three Key Contributions to Information Theory:**

1. **"A Mathematical Theory of Communication" (1948)** — Shannon developed information entropy as a measure of information content in a message, essentially founding the field of information theory and providing the blueprint for the digital era.

2. **Concept of the "bit"** — Shannon formally introduced the term "bit" as the fundamental unit of information, establishing the basis for all digital data representation and communication.

3. **Communication Theory of Secrecy Systems (1949)** — Shannon proved that any theoretically unbreakable cipher must share the same properties as the one-time pad, laying the mathematical foundation for modern cryptography and secure telecommunications.
